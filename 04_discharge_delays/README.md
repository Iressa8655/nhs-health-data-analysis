## What this analysis will cover

Discharge delay data counts how many patients are medically fit to leave hospital but cannot — because social care packages, Continuing Healthcare (CHC) assessments, or community step-down beds are not available.

This is **exit block**: one of the most widely cited structural causes of long A&E waits. The mechanism is direct:

> Patient fit for discharge → no social care package → stays in ward bed → no bed for new admission → A&E patient waits for a ward bed → A&E four-hour breach

Solving A&E performance without solving discharge delays is treating the symptom rather than the cause.

This analysis will answer:

- How many delayed days per month are attributable to NHS reasons vs social care reasons?
- Which ICS regions have the worst discharge delay rates?
- Is there a measurable correlation between discharge delay rates and A&E four-hour performance at the same trusts? (cross-referencing Project 01 data)
- Has the picture improved or worsened since 2024?

## Why this matters clinically

Patients who remain in hospital beyond medical need are exposed to hospital-acquired infections, deconditioning, falls, and pressure injuries. For older patients especially, prolonged unnecessary hospitalisation causes direct harm. Discharge delay is not just an efficiency problem — it is a patient safety problem.
