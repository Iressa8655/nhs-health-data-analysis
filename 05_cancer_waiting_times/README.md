# 05 — Cancer Waiting Times

**Status:** 📋 Planned  
**Standards:** 28-day Faster Diagnosis Standard (FDS); 62-day referral to treatment  
**Data:** NHS England — https://www.england.nhs.uk/statistics/statistical-work-areas/cancer-waiting-times/

---

## What this analysis will cover

Cancer waiting time standards exist because earlier diagnosis saves lives. The relationship between diagnostic delay and survival is well-established for many cancers — colorectal, ovarian, and lung cancers in particular show steep survival gradients by stage at diagnosis.

The two key standards:

- **28-day FDS:** patients with an urgent cancer referral (two-week wait) should receive a definitive diagnosis or ruling-out of cancer within 28 days
- **62-day standard:** patients should start treatment within 62 days of an urgent referral

This analysis will answer:

- What proportion of patients are being diagnosed within 28 days, and has this improved?
- Which cancer types have the worst 62-day treatment wait performance?
- Which trusts have the highest proportions of patients breaching the 62-day standard?
- Is there a relationship between diagnostic delays and the geography of deprivation (cross-referenced with IMD data)?

## Clinical context

In England, approximately 375,000 new cancer diagnoses are made each year. Late-stage diagnosis not only worsens individual outcomes — it also increases treatment costs significantly (complex surgery, palliative care, longer inpatient stays). Investment in early diagnosis pathways is both clinically and economically justified.
